10-year anniversary campaign

Light Up Salone

Celebrating ten years of Transform Salone by bringing solar power to four rural villages in Sierra Leone.

Donate to the campaign

In Sierra Leone, nearly nine in ten people live without electricity. In the rural villages we work with, when the sun goes down around 7pm, life simply stops. Children can’t study. Traders close up. Women and girls walking home are no longer safe. We want to change that.

The problem

  • About 89% of Sierra Leoneans have no mains electricity.
  • In the villages we support, darkness brings daily life to a halt.
  • Schoolwork, family businesses and community events all end at sunset.
  • Unlit paths make the walk home after dark dangerous, especially for women and girls.

The project

We’re installing around 80 solar streetlights across four partner villages, ranging from 20 to 60 households each. Two of these villages are home to schools we already support. We’re also adding solar lighting to our charity base, which lowers our running costs and improves safety on the compound.

The project is being delivered in partnership with the Rotary Club of Dover and uses equipment that can be maintained locally, so the impact lasts long after the lights are installed.

What it changes

  • Children can read and study in the evenings.
  • Traders, tailors and cooks can work and earn after dark.
  • Community meetings, worship and events can happen safely at night.
  • Women and girls walk home with real security for the first time.
